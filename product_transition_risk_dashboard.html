Key Findings & Recommendations
Finding 1

59% of SKUs require immediate transition action

14 SKUs are at EOL and 45 are in phase-out. Haircare carries the highest EOL exposure with 7 of 34 category SKUs at end-of-life. Transition planning should begin this week for all EOL-classified SKUs.

Finding 2

Supplier 1 is a single-source concentration risk

Supplier 1 holds 27% of all SKUs and 63% of those are in EOL or Phase-out status. Any supply disruption during the transition window would impact 17 SKUs simultaneously. Recommend dual-sourcing the 3 critical EOL SKUs.

Finding 3

74 SKUs have fewer than 7 days of supply

With a median days-of-supply of only 3.5 days, last purchase order cutoffs for EOL SKUs must be actioned within 2 weeks of this analysis to avoid unplanned stockouts during the transition window.

Finding 4

36% inspection failure rate understates quality risk

Only 23% of SKUs have passed inspection and 41% remain pending. For EOL-classified SKUs specifically, pending inspections should be prioritized before executing final replenishment orders to avoid receiving defective stock.

Finding 5

Sea transport is optimal for non-urgent transitions

Sea achieves the lowest average logistics cost at $418 vs $562 for Air. Road provides the best speed-cost balance at $553 with 4.7-day average shipping. Air should be reserved only for SKUs with days-of-supply under 7 days.

Finding 6

Active SKUs provide a 6-8 week new ramp window

The 41 active SKUs average 17+ days lead time, providing a viable 6-8 week ramp window for replacement SKU introduction before current EOL inventory depletes. New SKU launch timing should be synchronized to EOL dates in the Gantt.
